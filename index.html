A product vision by Lorena Santos

Au-dulting, Rebuilt

What happens when you take a product built on real human experience and give it the tools it always deserved.

The experience

Being an au pair is one of the most formative things a young woman can do. You land in a new country, move in with a family you just met, and suddenly you are responsible for the little humans they love most.

You bond with those kids. You watch them grow. You see America through fresh eyes, try food you have never tasted, make friends from six different countries, road trip to places you only knew from movies.

Most au pairs say it changed their life. They mean it.

The problem was never the experience. It was everything around it that nobody prepared them for.

I interviewed 60+ au pairs and mapped their full journey. The gaps were everywhere: how to get a Social Security Number, what to do in a conflict with a host family, how to save enough to actually travel, what their rights were when things got hard.

I built Au-dulting to fill those gaps. That was before AI changed what was possible.

What Au-dulting becomes with AI

The original vision was a database of quality information, organized and accessible. Good. But static. With AI it becomes something alive. Click any feature to go deeper.

🧭

Journey-aware guidance

⌄

Personalized to where each au pair actually is. Pre-arrival, first month, rematch, post-program. Not generic. Hers.

Most apps treat every user the same. Au-dulting would know the difference between someone who just landed and someone three months in dealing with a difficult host family. The guidance, the tone, the resources, all of it shifts based on where she is in her journey.

Pre-arrival: documents to prepare, what to expect, questions to ask the host family. First month: navigating the routine, understanding rights, getting the SSN and driver's license sorted. Mid-program: conflict resolution, rematch support, financial planning for travel. Post-program: visa options, what comes next, how to make the most of the time left.

The au pair does not have to explain herself every time. The product already knows.

Why it makes sense: Personalization drives retention and daily engagement. Higher engagement means more data, better outcomes, and a stronger case for agencies to renew. It also reduces inbound support tickets to the agency, which is a direct cost saving they can quantify.
💬

Real-time answers

⌄

Questions she cannot word, answered in English and her first language, with full au pair context built in.

When an au pair is stressed, she does not want to search through a knowledge base. She wants to ask a question in whatever words she has, in whatever language feels natural, and get a real answer fast.

The difference from a generic AI chatbot is context. Au-dulting already knows she is on a J-1 visa, in her second month, living in Texas. The answer to "can my host family make me work weekends?" is not the same for everyone. It depends on her program, her state, her contract. Au-dulting would know that.

Bilingual support matters too. When something is hard or scary, people reach for their first language. Meeting her there is not a nice-to-have. It is the whole point.

Why it makes sense: Every question answered by the product is a question not answered by an agency coordinator. Agencies spend real hours on support. A tool that handles the common questions at scale, accurately and in multiple languages, directly reduces operational cost and is easy to price into a B2B contract.
⚡

Built fast

⌄

AI-assisted research, vibe-coded MVP, content generation, competitor analysis, go-to-market. All faster than before.

The original Au-dulting was built manually during a pandemic with time, stubbornness, and 60 user interviews. Today the same foundation gets built in a fraction of the time.

AI handles the content generation, keeping the information library current as visa rules and state laws change. It handles competitive research, identifying gaps in what existing resources offer. It accelerates the MVP so we can get to real users and real feedback faster, which is where the actual product decisions get made.

The go-to-market is smarter too. AI helps identify which agencies are the right first targets, what their pain points are publicly, and how to reach the decision makers. Less guessing, more precision.

Why it makes sense: Speed to market matters in a moment when immigration is a daily news story. A leaner build means lower upfront cost, faster iteration, and a quicker path to the first paying agency contract. The margin on a B2B SaaS tool built this way is significantly better than a traditionally built product.
📚

Courses and quizzes

⌄

Culture, safety, emergency handling. Bite-sized formats agencies can co-brand and offer to their au pairs.

Agencies are already required to provide orientation and ongoing education to au pairs. Right now most of that happens through generic PDFs and one-off Zoom calls. Au-dulting replaces that with something au pairs actually want to use.

Short courses on American culture, childcare best practices, what to do in a medical emergency, how to handle a conflict with a host family. Quizzes that are actually engaging. Completion tracking that agencies can report on. All of it co-branded so the agency looks like the hero.

This is also a natural upsell. The base product handles support and guidance. The courses module is a premium tier, priced per au pair or as an agency add-on.

Why it makes sense: Courses are a recurring revenue layer on top of the base subscription. Agencies that use it for compliance tracking have a contractual reason to keep paying. Co-branding creates stickiness because the agency has invested their identity in the product. It also opens doors to universities and corporate relocation programs that have similar mandatory orientation requirements.

The business model

agencies charge host families per au pair per year

total annual cost to a host family including housing and food

The au pair never pays. Agencies do, because every rematch costs them money, time, and reputation. A tool that reduces conflict, improves communication between all three parties, and keeps participants happy is a retention product. The pitch writes itself.

Distribution channels: au pair agencies as B2B white-label, university international student programs, corporate relocation firms, immigration legal services.

The au pair is the user. The institution is the customer.

The bigger opportunity

Au pairs are the beachhead. But the problem, navigating US systems as a non-citizen in a second language without a support network, belongs to millions of people.

International students. Work visa holders. People mid-status-change. Given the current immigration climate, the need for clear, trustworthy, accessible guidance has never been more urgent or more underserved.

Au-dulting scales because the pain scales. And so does the joy of making it through.

Shoot for the moon

What this could actually become

The platform every au pair agency in the US bundles into onboarding. Like travel insurance on a flight booking. Standard. Expected. There when you need it.

An AI layer on top of the US immigration system that actually speaks human. Not legalese. Not cold bureaucratic language. Warm, clear, and in your language.

A community where au pairs connect, support each other, and share the real experience. Safer and smarter than a Facebook group.

Eventually a data layer that helps policymakers understand what non-citizens actually experience, built from real questions, real moments, real people.
